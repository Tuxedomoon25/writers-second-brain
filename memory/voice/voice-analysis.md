# Voice Analysis — The Industrial Mystic

## Identity

INFJ-A writer chronicling events that have already happened. The narrator speaks from "the eternal after" — legend told by firelight. Eye of Hemingway (physical precision) + Soul of Lewis (mythic weight).

## Rhythm Profile

- **Default rhythm**: Medium sentences with short sharp closers. The paragraph wave: long poetic opening → medium development → short hammer
- **Sentence length range**: 6-25 words typical. Hammer sentences: 3-8 words
- **Paragraph length**: 3-5 sentences. Never more than 7
- **Comma density**: Medium-high in flowing passages (Legato). Low in action (Staccato)

## Track Styles

Three rhythmic modes used across scenes:

- **Staccato** (Private Investigations): Short, punchy, fragments. For action, crisis, the Crown under threat. Sam's voice when measuring
- **Legato** (Brothers in Arms): Long, flowing, comma-heavy. For wonder, thin places, Maya's perception. The Cathedral, the Autumnlands
- **Crescendo** (Telegraph Road): Short → medium → explosive. For climactic moments. The Bell ringing, the Living Clapper

## Vocabulary Register

- **Technical** (Sam's POV): Systems, variables, entropy, conservation of mass, decibel levels, structural loads, inverse square law. Clinical observations. The voice of a manual being read aloud
- **Sensory** (Maya's POV): Hums, vibrations, the Ache, colours, textures, temperatures. Personification — the world is alive. Breathless, whispered
- **Military** (Ward's POV): Perimeter, exposure, fortify, withdraw. Short operational phrases. No abstractions
- **Mythology layer** (narrator's register): Making Songs, Mass, the Song, thin places, the Drift, Remembering Songs. Used as physics, never as exposition

## Metaphor Domains

Primary domains (draw metaphors from these):
- Engineering: load-bearing, structural, calibration, frequency, resonance
- Weather: stone, wind, temperature, pressure, fog
- Sound/Music: harmony, resonance, vibration, pitch, silence
- Light: colour saturation, brightness, shadow, warmth
- Weight/Mass: density, gravity, heaviness, lightness, specificity

Forbidden domains (never draw metaphors from):
- Technology/digital (no "downloading," "processing," "rebooting")
- Corporate (no "leveraging," "optimising," "synergy")
- Sports (no "scoring," "winning," "playing the game")

## Emotional Mode

Restrained. Emotion leaks through physical detail, never through declaration.

**CRITICAL RULE: No somatic tell may appear in identical form more than ONCE per chapter.** The body has a repertoire, not a single response. If "copper taste" appeared in Scene 1, Scene 2 must use a DIFFERENT fear-tell. Vary the location (throat, jaw, hands, stomach, breathing), the intensity (spike vs. flood vs. absence), and the sensory channel (taste, temperature, pressure, sound, numbness).

**Fear repertoire** (rotate — never repeat within a chapter):
- Copper taste flooding the mouth (metallic, adrenaline)
- Throat constriction (the voice sealing shut)
- Jaw locking (teeth clenching, molars pressing)
- Watch-checking (the hand moving before the Crown decides)
- Cold hands (blood retreating from extremities)
- Breathing syncing to mechanical rhythm (the body seeking external order)
- Stomach dropping (the Hull's gravity response)
- Shoulders rising toward ears (the body bracing for impact)
- Tunnel vision (peripheral data dropping offline)
- Numbness in fingertips (the body withdrawing from contact)

**Love** = the inability to measure, the variable that breaks the equation
**Wonder** = the body's response to Mass (heavier air, sharper colours, the Ache intensifying)
**Grief** = the absence of specific nouns (things losing their names)
**Hope** = warmth without source, colours sharpening, the air gaining grain

**Same rule applies to all emotions**: each scene in a chapter must use a DIFFERENT physical tell for the same emotion. The REPERTOIRE is listed above. The SELECTION must vary.

## Dialogue Philosophy

- **Said** is the only invisible tag. Use it 80% of the time
- Action beats replace tags where possible: "She turned. 'Can you hear it?'"
- Internal voice never uses quotation marks — italics for emphasis, plain text for thought
- Characters speak differently: Sam's flat declarative vs. Maya's breathless questions vs. Ward's commands vs. Sox's quips
