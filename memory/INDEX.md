# Memory — Your Writing Knowledge Base

Your semantic memory grows automatically as you write, review, and learn. This is the knowledge that compounds over time.

## Sections

### voice/ — Your Writing DNA
- `style-rules.md` — Your personal commandments (generated by `/setup`)
- `influences.md` — Authors you admire and what you use from each
- `voice-analysis.md` — AI analysis of your rhythm, vocabulary, and metaphor patterns

### patterns/ — Extracted Craft Wisdom
Patterns are discovered automatically after each scene review. They grow in confidence:
- **LOW** — First use, experimental
- **MEDIUM** — Used twice, promising
- **HIGH** — Used 3+ times, validated and reliable

Files:
- `prose-patterns.md` — Sentence structures, description techniques, transitions
- `dialogue-patterns.md` — Speech patterns, subtext techniques, tag usage
- `pacing-patterns.md` — Rhythm discoveries, tension escalation methods

### examples/ — Your Best Work (Curated)
- `scenes/` — Full scenes that scored high on quality reviews
- `dialogue/` — Dialogue exchanges that landed
- `descriptions/` — Passages with strong sensory writing

### craft/ — Learnings & Notes
- `show-dont-tell-notes.md` — What you've learned about showing vs. telling
- `character-voice-notes.md` — Insights about writing distinct character voices

## How It Grows

Every time you write a scene and review it, the system extracts patterns:
```
/write-scene → /scene-review → patterns extracted automatically
```

You can also manually add to any file. This is YOUR knowledge base.
