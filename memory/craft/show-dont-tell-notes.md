# Show Don't Tell — Craft Notes

> Learnings about showing vs. telling from 15 scenes. Updated 2026-03-20.

## Core Technique: Systems Failure as Character Arc

Sam doesn't change internally — his *systems* fail. Compass, clock, physics, conservation of mass — all progressively unreliable. This IS how you show cognitive/emotional breakdown without naming it.

- Compass needle spins → navigation fails (Ch4 Sc1)
- Watch stops → time covenant broken (Ch5 Sc1)
- Conservation of mass violated → "It's *matter*!" (Ch5 Sc1)
- Electromagnetic spectrum exceeded → categorisation fails (Ch5 Sc3)

**The rule**: When a boy's instruments fail, his world fails. No psychology needed.

## Somatic Rotation (Rule 11) — What Works

The constraint of never repeating a somatic tell within a chapter forces *specificity*. Each new sensation becomes a fresh data point. By Ch5, copper taste signals danger in a way that would be stale if reused.

**Best tells by emotion:**

| Emotion | Strongest Shows (from scored scenes) |
|---------|--------------------------------------|
| Fear | Copper taste, throat constriction, cold hands (blood retreating) |
| Shame | Face hot / capillary dilation, "the body broadcasting what his mouth would never admit" |
| Grief | Phantom weight in empty hand, "the phantom architecture of a thing erased" |
| Wonder | Chest pressure (sternum vibrating), colours sharpening, air thickening |
| Dread | Stomach dropping ("a weight falling from behind his navel"), tunnel vision |
| Love | Warmth without source, Maya's hand on bare arm, the variable that breaks the equation |

**What NOT to do**: Never label the emotion in the same paragraph as the somatic tell. The tell IS the emotion. Adding "He felt afraid" after "copper flooded his tongue" kills both.

## The Iceberg Hierarchy (Effectiveness Ranking)

From 15 scenes of scoring, these show-don't-tell methods rank:

1. **Somatic tells** (9/10 consistently) — Body speaks, Crown is silent
2. **Object behaviour** (9/10) — The machine confesses what Sam won't
3. **Systems failure** (9/10) — Instruments break = worldview breaks
4. **Action-as-emotion** (8-9/10) — Sam builds the cannon instead of processing fear
5. **Environmental reflection** (8/10) — Mist advances when singing weakens
6. **Dialogue subtext** (8/10) — What isn't said carries the weight

## Common Failure Mode

The one pattern that drops scores to 8: **naming an emotion in narrator voice then showing it**. Example from early drafts: "Humiliation burned through him. His face was hot." The fix: cut the first sentence. "His face was hot" IS the humiliation. Trust the reader.
