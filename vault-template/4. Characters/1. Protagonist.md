## Psychology

### Core Wound

> What happened to them — or what was absent — that created the Lie? The wound doesn't need to be dramatic. Sometimes the quietest wounds cut deepest. A parent who was present but never really saw them. A moment of shame they never processed.

[Write it here]

### Desire (Conscious)

> What do they think they want? This is what they'd tell you if you asked. It's usually a symptom of the wound, not a cure.

[Write it here]

### Need (Unconscious)

> What do they actually need? This is the Truth they can't see yet. The story is the journey from Desire to Need.

[Write it here]

### Fear

> What do they avoid at all costs? Fear is the guardian of the Lie. As long as they avoid this, the Lie stays safe.

[Write it here]

### Coping Mechanism

> How do they protect themselves? Do they withdraw? Control? Perform? People-please? This is the behavior your reader will see in Act 1.

[Write it here]

## Voice Profile

### Internal Voice

> Close your eyes and listen to this character think. Are their thoughts structured and logical, or chaotic and associative? Do they think in images or in arguments? What words would they never use?

[Write it here]

### Dialogue Voice

> Now listen to them speak. Short, clipped sentences or long, winding ones? Do they use technical words, poetic language, or street slang? Do they finish their sentences? Do they say what they mean, or talk around it?

[Write it here]

### Emotional Signature

> How does this character's body show emotion? Do they clench their jaw when angry? Hold their breath when scared? Tap their fingers when anxious? This is your show-don't-tell anchor — the physical language that replaces "she felt sad."

[Write it here]

### Awareness Filter

> What details does this character notice? A mechanic notices squeaks and rattles. A painter notices light and shadow. A mother notices small dangers. What your character notices reveals who they are without you having to explain it.

[Write it here]

## Arc

### Starting State (Act 1)

> Where does your character start? Not physically — emotionally. What mask are they wearing? What are they avoiding? Show the Lie in full operation.

[Write it here]

### Catalyst

> What happens that cracks the Lie open? This is the inciting incident — not necessarily the biggest event, but the one that makes the old way of living impossible.

[Write it here]

### Transformation (Act 2-3)

> How do they change? What do they lose? What do they gain? The Lie doesn't die easily — it fights back. Show the struggle.

[Write it here]

### Ending State

> Who do they become? Or — equally valid — who do they refuse to become? Not every arc ends in growth. Some end in deeper commitment to the Lie. That's tragedy, and it's just as powerful.

[Write it here]
