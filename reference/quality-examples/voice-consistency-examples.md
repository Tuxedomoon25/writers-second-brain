# Character Voice Consistency — Quality Level Examples

The same scene from the POV of an **engineer** (logical, precise, notices mechanical details). She walks into an old church.

---

## Level 6/10 — Adequate

> The church was beautiful and old. Elena looked around at the impressive architecture and felt a sense of wonder. The stained glass windows were colorful and the ceiling was very high. She thought about how skilled the builders must have been to create such a magnificent structure.

**Why 6/10:** Generic observations that could come from anyone. No engineering lens. "Beautiful", "impressive", "magnificent" are evaluative words that don't match an engineer's analytical voice. The character's profession is invisible.

---

## Level 8/10 — Good

> Elena tilted her head back. The nave ceiling rose at least fifteen meters — pointed arches distributing the load down through the columns to foundations she couldn't see but could estimate. Limestone, probably. Maybe sandstone in the older sections. The stained glass threw colored light across the floor in patterns that shifted as clouds moved outside, and she found herself calculating the angle of incidence without meaning to.

**Why 8/10:** The engineering voice is present — she estimates structural loads, identifies stone types, calculates angles. "At least fifteen meters" shows her instinct to measure. The involuntary calculation ("without meaning to") reveals character. Small weakness: "she found herself" is slightly distanced.

---

## Level 10/10 — Excellent

> Fifteen meters. Maybe sixteen. Elena's neck ached from the angle but she couldn't stop mapping the load paths — stone pressing down on stone, the pointed arches turning gravity into lateral thrust, the flying buttresses outside (she'd seen them from the parking lot) catching that thrust and channeling it into the ground. Eight hundred years of compression. She pressed her thumb against the nearest column. Cool. Dry. Not a crack she could find. Someone had known what they were doing. Someone with a slide rule and a lifetime and no finite element software had gotten it right on the first try, and it was still standing, and her apartment building with its steel frame and computer-modeled joints had already needed repairs twice.

**Why 10/10:** Every observation comes through the engineering filter — load paths, lateral thrust, compression, finite element software. The character's admiration is expressed through technical understanding, not generic wonder. The comparison to her modern apartment building reveals both professional knowledge and emotional response (humility, respect) without naming either. The voice is utterly consistent — this could only be Elena.
