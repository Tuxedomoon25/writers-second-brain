> This is Timo's specific voice profile — generated by his `/setup` interview. Yours will be completely different, reflecting YOUR influences, YOUR rhythm, YOUR metaphor domains.

## Voice Profile

- **Voice Name:** The Industrial Mystic
- **Rhythm:** Mostly medium sentences with short, sharp closers. Long sentences build; short sentences break.
- **Metaphor Domains:** Engineering, weather, stone, light, sound/music
- **Register:** Precise but not clinical. Every word earns its place.
- **Emotional Mode:** Restrained — emotion leaks through physical detail, never announced.

## Core Influences

| Author | What Timo Uses |

| C.S. Lewis | Mythic weight. The sense that ordinary moments are windows to something larger. Warmth beneath the discipline. |
| Mark Knopfler (music) | One instrument carries the song. One object carries the scene. The tactile anchor. |

## What This Voice Sounds Like

> The Keep was quieter than it had any right to be. Sam pressed his palm against the stone wall and counted heartbeats — his own, steady at sixty-two, and something underneath. A pulse in the granite that wasn't geological. He pulled his hand back and checked his watch. The second hand moved. The minute hand moved. Time was behaving.

> But the silence wasn't.

> It had a texture now — thick, like the air before a bell rings. Maya would have called it pressure. Sox would have called it nothing. Sam called it data he couldn't instrument, and that was worse than either.

## Why This Voice Works FOR TIMO

- **Engineering lens** (Sam's POV) — counting heartbeats, checking instruments, calling experience "data"
- **Two layers** — physical (pulse in granite, second hand moving) + mythic (silence with texture, air before a bell rings)
- **Short paragraph punch** — "But the silence wasn't." on its own line
- **Tactile anchor** — the watch
- **Show don't tell** — anxiety shown through counting and checking, never named
