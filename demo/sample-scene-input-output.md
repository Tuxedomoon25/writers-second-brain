## The Scene

A woman named Lena, a marine biologist, returns to her childhood home after her father's funeral. She enters his study for the first time since she was a child.

## Version 1: Raw AI Output (No Rules)

> Lena stepped into her father's study, feeling a wave of emotions wash over her. The room was exactly as she remembered it — dusty bookshelves lined the walls, and the familiar scent of pipe tobacco lingered in the air. She felt overwhelmed by memories of her childhood, when she used to sit on his lap while he read to her from his collection of maritime books.
>
> "I can't believe he's gone," she whispered, tears forming in her eyes. The desk was cluttered with papers and old photographs, and she noticed a beautiful antique compass sitting prominently on top of a stack of letters. It was a testament to his lifelong passion for the sea — a passion he had clearly passed down to her.
>
> She picked up the compass and examined it carefully. It was a remarkable piece, intricately designed with delicate engravings. She realized that this was probably the same compass he had shown her as a child, the one that had first sparked her fascination with the ocean. The weight of it in her hand was comforting, additionally serving as a tangible connection to the man she had loved so deeply.

**What's wrong:**
- Emotions named: "feeling a wave of emotions," "felt overwhelmed," "comforting"
- Telling: "She realized," "She felt"
- AI vocabulary: "testament to," "additionally," "remarkable"
- Cliches: "tears forming in her eyes," "wave of emotions"
- Purple prose: "intricately designed with delicate engravings"
- Significance inflation: "a testament to his lifelong passion"

## Version 2: After Quality Gates (Rules Applied)

**Rules active:** Simple past tense. Show don't tell. Zero adverbs. One metaphor per paragraph. "Said" only for dialogue tags.

> The study door stuck on the carpet, same as it always had. Lena pushed harder and the wood gave. Dust moved in the light from the hallway — slow, deliberate, as if the room had been holding its breath.
>
> The pipe rack on the windowsill still held three briars. She counted them without meaning to. The leather chair had cracked along the armrest where his elbow had worn the same groove for thirty years. She ran her thumb along the crack and felt the stuffing underneath — dry, compressed, shaped to a man who wasn't coming back.
>
> On the desk, under a stack of yellow-edged letters, a compass. Brass, tarnished green at the hinge. She picked it up and the needle swung north, then settled. Eight years old. His hands covering hers, the two of them on the back porch, and he'd said — what had he said? Something about the needle always knowing. She closed her fist around the case. The brass was cold. The needle pointed toward the window, toward the sea she could hear but not see.

**What improved:**
- Zero named emotions — grief shown through physical detail (cracked leather, dry stuffing, counting pipes)
- Specific sensory anchors (brass tarnished green, dry compressed stuffing, cold metal)
- Memory woven through physical action, not narrated
- "She counted them without meaning to" — reveals character through behavior
- Final image (compass pointing toward the sea) earns its metaphor
